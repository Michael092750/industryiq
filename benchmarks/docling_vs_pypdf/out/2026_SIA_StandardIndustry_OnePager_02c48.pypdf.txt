Semiconductors are the brains of modern electronics, enabling advances in health care, 
communications, computing, defense, aerospace, transportation, infrastructure, and energy. 
Chips are also giving rise to game-changing technologies of the future, including artificial 
intelligence, quantum computing, and advanced wireless networks. A globally competitive 
U.S. semiconductor industry will allow America to contest global challenges, boost the 
economy, enhance national security, and lead the technology race of the 21st century.
Rapid innovation has enabled the semiconductor industry to produce exponentially more advanced products at 
a lower cost. Today, commercially available advanced chips include transistors with features as small as 2 or 3 
nanometers, just 5 atoms wide and 30,000 times thinner than a human hair .
