## About This Report

As the world, and the United States in particular, confronts the challenges of economic mobility, health and longevity, and new frontiers of technology and human well-being, have we designed our social and economic systems to effectively address these convergences? RAND is addressing these questions through an effort called Social and Economic Policy Rethink to transform the approach to solving social and economic policy problems. The broader volume encompasses seven publications that describe key social and economic stakes for policy on artificial intelligence (AI) adoption, examine how policymakers can account for rapid AI capabilities development and adoption in and across sectors, and showcase how industry and policy leaders can responsibly meet the challenges of an era of transformative AI.

In this report, we present the AI Capabilities Framework, which serves as a tool for understanding existing AI applications, anticipating future developments, and emphasizing the need for systematic evaluation of the potential policy challenges, opportunities, and uncertainties surrounding AI adoption. The report focuses on AI's transformative potential across four specific sectors: financial services, climate and energy, health care, and transportation. Key insights include identifying sectorspecific risks and challenges, along with suggested policy responses to mitigate these issues while balancing potential benefits. The framework aids in identifying and responding to AI-related risks beyond the four selected sectors by offering a structured approach to align policy solutions with AI's evolving capabilities. We also suggest integrating additional frameworks, such as the Comprehensive Mapping Protocol for Anticipating and Adapting to Systemic Shocks (COMPASS) approach and other RAND Strategic Rethink efforts, to enhance the understanding and management of systemic AI risks.

## Chapter 1 Introduction

The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence (AI) has profound implications for lawmakers, policymakers, and private sector leaders across various industries. As a concept, AI is often discussed in the singular; however, the term encompasses an expanding variety of broad capabilities and the way in which these capabilities are adopted. In this report, we focus on the adoption of this broad class of AI technologies, including machine learning (ML), deep learning (DL), natural language processing (NLP), large language models (LLMs), and generative AI (GenAI). 1 Specifically, we examine AI applications in key sectors-health care, financial services, climate and energy, and transportation-that are integral to societal well-being and economic stability. Our analysis of AI applications in these sectors not only demonstrates the transformative potential of AI but also highlights the distinct challenges and risks that arise both within individual sectors and across sectors. We examine the following questions, which are important for understanding how to harness AI effectively and responsibly:

- What are the existing capabilities of AI in health care, financial services, climate and energy, and transportation, and what near-term applications are likely to emerge?
- What are the primary challenges and risks posed by the adoption of AI within each of these sectors, and what potential policy responses can be developed to address these issues?
- What are the key cross-sectoral challenges arising from the integration of AI across industries, and what frameworks or guidelines can assist policymakers and stakeholders in understanding and addressing these challenges?

The methodology for this research involved a comprehensive literature review, which we outline in Chapter 2. We developed the AI Capabilities Framework to guide our assessment of existing AI applications and help anticipate future developments, challenges, and risks. In Chapter 3, we detail the foundations and applications of this framework. In Chapter 4, we use the AI Capabilities Framework to examine the uptake of AI in our selected sectors. In Chapter 5, we explore the challenges and potential policy responses related to AI integration within each sector, focusing on such issues as workforce transformation and systemic risks. Finally, in Chapter 6, we address cross-sectoral challenges and responses, emphasizing the interconnected nature of AI adoption and the importance of developing comprehensive frameworks to manage the associated risks effectively.

1 ML is an AI subset that enables computers to learn from data without explicit programming, allowing them to identify patterns and make predictions. DL is a subset of ML that uses multilayered neural networks to learn complex patterns from large datasets, mimicking the human brain. NLP is an AI field focused on enabling computers to understand, process, and generate human language (text and speech). LLMs are advanced NLP models with billions of parameters, trained on vast text data to generate and comprehend humanlike text. GenAI refers to AI systems that use DL and LLMs to create new, original content (including text, images, and audio) rather than just analyzing existing data. Additionally, artificial general intelligence is a theoretical AI system without a consensus definition that can perform most or all cognitive tasks at the level of human capability.

## Chapter 2

## Methodology

The methodology for this research primarily involved a comprehensive literature review conducted by researchers with domain knowledge of the selected sectors. The review was complemented by our development of the AI Capabilities Framework, which we used to contextualize and analyze the insights obtained from the literature.

## Source Selection

We focused on a diverse variety of sources-including peer-reviewed academic articles, industry reports, and policy papers-that examine existing AI applications, adoption rates, and sector-specific challenges. These sources were selected based on their relevance, credibility, and contribution to understanding the transformative potential of AI within the focal sectors of health care, financial services, climate and energy, and transportation.

## Analysis

We extracted key themes related to AI capabilities and their implications and categorized findings by sector-specific use cases, challenges, risks, and policy intervention opportunities. The AI Capabilities Framework was developed to synthesize these insights, providing a structured approach to assess existing AI applications and anticipate future developments and challenges.

## Limitations

Although the literature review prioritized recency and relevance, it is important to note that the rapidly evolving nature of AI means that some emerging applications might not be fully captured. New developments can arise faster than they can be documented in academic and industry sources, potentially leading to gaps in the analysis.

## Chapter 3

## AI Capabilities Framework

AI stands at an inflection point; advances that have been achieved over the past few years suggest that the technology possesses capabilities that could transform major sectors of the economy and society. 2 These advances encompass a broad and expanding set of capabilities that include ML, DL, NLP, LLMs, and GenAI. 3  To measure and track this progress, leading AI labs and research institutions have begun proposing structured frameworks (discussed in the next section) that recognize the ways in which AI's trajectory will have profound implications across industries. 4 This shift toward more-systematic evaluation reflects both the accelerating pace of AI development and a growing awareness that different sectors of the economy might experience AI's impact in distinct ways. As AI capabilities advance from basic language processing to sophisticated reasoning and autonomous operation, developing a unified framework designed to understand and anticipate these transformative changes becomes increasingly vital for policymakers, industry, and researchers.

We begin this chapter by outlining a framework for understanding existing AI applications and anticipating future developments. We also provide an overview of the potential transformative uses of AI across various sectors, including financial services, climate and energy, health care, and transportation. Additionally, we briefly address the key issues and considerations associated with AI implementation in these sectors.

## Understanding AI Capability Frameworks

The rapid evolution of AI capabilities necessitates a clear framework for tracking progress and anticipating future developments. The three frameworks examined in this section represent the mostrecent and influential approaches from AI research organizations currently at the forefront of generalpurpose AI development.
